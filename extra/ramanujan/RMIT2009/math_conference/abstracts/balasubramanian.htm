An identity of Ramanujam in  Riemann zeta function

Since it is known that the prime number theorem is equivalent to the nonvanishing of the Riemann zeta function on the 1 line, the proof of the nonvanishing becomes an important aspect of study in the theory of Riemann zeta function. The usual proof of the nonvanishing is based on the inequality 3 + 4 cos x + cos ( 2x) is nonnegative. Ingham observed that the nonvanishing of the zeta function on the 1 line can be deduced fom an identity of Ramanujan. We shall discuss the ramification of the observations of Ramanujan.
