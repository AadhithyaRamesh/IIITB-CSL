On a generalized basic series and new combinatorial interpretations of Goellnitz-Gordon identities

We interpret a generalized basic series as a generating function for certain restricted Bender-Knuth matrices. This in combination with recent work of Agarwal and Rana leads to an infinite class of 5-way combinatorial identities. In some cases we get even 6-way combinatorial identities. We discuss three such particular cases and obtain fifteen new combinatorial interpretations for each of the two Goellnitz-Gordon partition identities and an identity due to Goellnitz.
